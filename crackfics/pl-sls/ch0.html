A Goodstein Sequence

There were many reasons behind Bill Hawks’ presence on this precise day, at this precise hour, about to change History in what could be the most literal sense possible.

For the first, and perhaps the last time in his life, the scientist could experience this ever so rare sense of nausea that comes neither from sickness nor from anticipation per se, but rather from the realization of how crucial the next few moments would be; from the sheer weight of the power he had in his hands; from the intoxicating knowledge that in this very moment, he was the one in control of not just his own destiny, but that of many others.

There were, once again, many reasons for him to be there right then. Progress was one of them; the one he wished to keep upfront, and the only one he wished others to see. Ambition was another; the one he would try to justify as a good reason, as long as it remained the means to an end, rather than an end all by itself. And then, greed… The one he pretended did not exist.
