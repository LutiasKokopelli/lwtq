Stable Like Sand

September 28, 1953, Gressenheller University of London. Isolated in their laboratory, two scientists are about to reach a historic breakthrough.

As the sand flows inexorably through the pierced glass, their quest comes ever so close to its climax: one yearns for glory, the other for knowledge.

The machine is running, and it is time for a small grain to disrupt the gears. History will be revolutionised indeed; but not through the means they expected.
