Preliminary Notes and Disclaimers

“Unwound/Lost Future takes place in winter 1963/1964” has been chosen as the main point of reference in this timeline. The source behind this is an artwork/concept art from 2008, which is present on the official Japanese website of Unwound/Lost Future, and on the Japanese & US box arts of the game.

This is a semi-arbitrary choice whose canonicity can be debated. American (left) and Japanese (right) Box Arts:

Luke and Layton are obstructing the view, but it can still be seen that the clock is the same as the one from the official website.



The in-game version of the clock has no digits:

Within the game itself, the digits have been removed and replaced with random buttons; this is why the canonicity of this date is up for debate.

 It has been chosen here for the sake of convenience, but it should be relatively easy for you to change the year if it does not suit your personal views.

Here is a close-up of the date shown on the artwork. Unwound/Lost Future is about time travel, between “the present” and “ten years later”: it can be assumed that these moving digits are meant to reflect this. The rightmost two digits are changing, to give the year(s) 『１９ＸＹ』. From this, we can deduce that:

  • Ｘ alternates between 6 and 7. The number 7 is easily recogniseable. Furthermore, we can deduce that the digit above it is 6 and not 8: the series’ aesthetic was confirmed to have been inspired by the 1960sThe World of Professor Layton artbook, I’m pretty sure? Please send me a photo/screenshot if you have one!, and the DS version of Curious Village contains an unused, now obsolete artwork reading “Year 1960” (more on that later). Therefore, it is safe to conclude that “the present” is set in the 1960s, and that the (fake) “ten years later” is set in the 1970s.

    • This also helps clear up the problem behind the last digit:

  • Ｙ alternates between 3 and 4. Once again, 4 is unmistakable; as for whether the number above it is a 3 or a 5, I made the assumption that all four digits are displayed in the same order: in the previous case, 6 was displayed above 7, therefore 3 should be the number displayed above 4, and not 5.
  • In conclusion: This image seems to be leaning towards the message “Your destination is the year 1973/1974 (not really but spoilers).” And thus, Unwound/Lost Future took place in 1963/1964.

    • In practicality, it should not be a 100% certainty; but since 3 and 4 were the numbers chosen by the artist, I decided to go along with it.

  • Furthermore, I figured that Unwound/Lost Future was more likely to take place in winter (between roughly November 1963 and February 1964), since it snows at the end.

Small digression: December 31, 1963 would be quite poetic for the events of Unwound/Lost Future, as another explanation for the change between 3 and 4; but this is just a personal opinion and we will not consider it in this timeline.

An alternative to this point of reference could be this unused artwork datamined from Curious Village, which would imply that CV would have taken place in the year 1960. This unused artwork has been ignored here in favor of the 1963/1964 artwork due to the latter being more recent, as well as still publicly available on the official website and merchandise rather than hidden and unused.

If a character’s age is officially revealed in relation to a given story, then it is assumed until proven impossible by subsequent evidence that their age remains constant during the entire duration of that story. This is especially relevant in the case of Azran Legacy, which takes place over the span of multiple months. For example, Emmy’s age is stated to be 26 in AL: it is thus assumed that her age is 26 from the moment she embarks on the Bostonius, up to the moment when she leaves Layton and the credits roll.
